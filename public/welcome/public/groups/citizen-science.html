Citizen Science for a Repairable Planet

Join the work. Share the knowledge. Build what lasts.

We are building a citizen-led research and action network focused on practical solutions to climate stress, resource waste, and ecological breakdown. This is not an academic exercise and it’s not a marketing campaign—it’s a working collaboration between people who care enough to measure, document, test, and improve the systems we all depend on.

Our approach blends hands-on experimentation, open documentation, and collective pressure—because real change happens when evidence, practice, and public accountability reinforce one another.

Whether you’re a builder, mapper, researcher, organizer, tinkerer, student, or simply curious, there is a place for you here.

What We Mean by “Citizen Science”

Citizen science means:

  • Measuring real conditions where people actually live

  • Sharing results openly and reproducibly

  • Learning across disciplines instead of siloing expertise

  • Designing solutions that can be built, repaired, and improved locally

You do not need formal credentials. You do need curiosity, care, and a willingness to document what you observe.

  • Community-scale reuse systems

  • Resilience during outages and floods

Citizen science angle:
Create shared maps, audit local systems, and surface hidden dependencies. This knowledge empowers communities and improves public accountability.

How Working Groups Collaborate

Each focus area operates as a working group, but no group works in isolation.

We actively encourage:

  • Cross-posting findings

  • Shared tools and methodologies

  • Joint projects (e.g. solar + water, plastics + policy)

  • Peer review from outside your discipline

This network effect is the point.

Ways to Participate

You can contribute by:

  • Joining a working group

  • Sharing observations, measurements, or experiments

  • Building or testing designs

  • Mapping local systems

  • Writing guides or documentation

  • Reviewing and improving others’ work

  • Helping coordinate or facilitate a group

Small contributions matter. Consistent contributions matter more.

What We Value

  • Transparency over perfection

  • Repair over replacement

  • Evidence over opinion

  • Collaboration over credit

  • Long-term thinking over short-term wins

This is slow work done carefully—but it scales through shared effort.

Get Involved

Choose a working group that resonates with you, or propose a new one if you see a gap. Introduce yourself, share what you’re curious about, and start contributing at a pace that’s sustainable.

You don’t need permission to begin—just a willingness to document what you learn and share it openly.
